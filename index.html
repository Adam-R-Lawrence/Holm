Research

My research entails computational mechanics and modelling, mainly in free-surface hydrodynamics, with additional applications in photopolymerization and additive manufacturing.

Background

Hydraulic engineering with computing depth

I am from Melbourne, Australia, where I completed undergraduate and master's study in environmental engineering, specializing in hydraulic engineering with a minor in computer science. I am also an AUKUS fellow through the Australian-American Association.

Software Portfolio

Research tools under active development

Torrentem

Parallel CPU/GPU multiphysics solver for PDEs, free-surface hydrodynamics, stabilized FEM, and manufacturing simulations.

 Ostium

Geospatial data processing and integration tooling designed to prepare domains for Torrentem.

 Vadum

Visualization tooling for reading, inspecting, and communicating results produced by Torrentem.

 Stratum

G-code generation and build preparation for DLP and SLA photopolymerization workflows.
